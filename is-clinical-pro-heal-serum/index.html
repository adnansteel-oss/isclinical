1. L-Ascorbic Acid (Vitamin C)

The Pro-Heal Serum uses a highly stabilized, professional-concentration form of L-Ascorbic Acid — the only form of Vitamin C clinically proven to penetrate the dermal layers effectively. At this concentration, it actively neutralizes free radicals, inhibits melanin overproduction, and supports the skin's natural collagen synthesis pathways, making it foundational for both repair and prevention.

2. Retinol (Vitamin A)

Retinol is the gold standard for skin renewal and one of the most extensively studied anti-aging ingredients in dermatology. In the Pro-Heal formulation, retinol is incorporated at a therapeutic concentration that accelerates cellular turnover, promotes the production of new collagen, and visibly reduces fine lines and skin textural irregularities over time.

3. Vitamin E (Tocopherol)

Vitamin E works in direct synergy with Vitamin C, recycling and extending the antioxidant activity of L-Ascorbic Acid within the skin. It also provides direct conditioning benefits, supporting the lipid barrier and reinforcing the skin's natural moisture retention capacity.

4. Olive Leaf Extract

Olive Leaf Extract is a powerful polyphenol with clinically documented anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. In this formulation, it plays a critical role in calming reactive and inflamed skin while providing additional antioxidant reinforcement that supports the overall protective efficacy of the serum.

5. Hyaluronic Acid

Hyaluronic Acid delivers immediate and sustained hydration by drawing moisture into the upper layers of the skin. This is particularly critical for compromised or post-procedure skin, which often suffers significant moisture loss during the healing process.

How to Use iS Clinical Pro-Heal Serum

Cleanse First: Apply to a clean, dry face after cleansing morning and evening.
Dosage: Dispense 2–4 drops and gently press the serum into the skin using the fingertips. Do not rub.
Wait Before Layering: Allow 60–90 seconds for the serum to absorb fully before applying a moisturizer.
SPF Is Non-Negotiable (AM): Follow with a broad-spectrum SPF 50+ every morning, especially post-procedure.
Post-Procedure Protocol: If used after a clinical treatment, apply only as directed by your treating physician or esthetician, as the timing of reintroduction matters for the healing process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use Pro-Heal Serum immediately after a laser treatment?
Timing depends on the intensity of the procedure. For superficial treatments, your clinician may clear you to begin using it within 24–48 hours. For more aggressive resurfacing, reintroduction is typically delayed by 7–14 days. Always follow your treating physician's post-care instructions.
Is Pro-Heal Serum suitable for daily use?
Yes. It is formulated for twice-daily use as part of a standard morning and evening skincare routine.
Can I use Pro-Heal with iS Clinical Active Serum?
These two serums serve different purposes. It is generally recommended to use Active Serum in the morning and Pro-Heal in the evening, or to alternate based on your current skin priorities.
Is this serum suitable for rosacea-prone skin?
Yes. The anti-inflammatory botanical blend — particularly Olive Leaf Extract — makes Pro-Heal Serum one of the more tolerable clinical serums for rosacea-prone complexions.
