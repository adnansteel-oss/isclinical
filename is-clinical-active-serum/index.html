1. Glycolic Acid (Sugarcane Extract)

Glycolic acid is the gold-standard AHA in clinical exfoliation. At the concentration used in Active Serum, it effectively dissolves the bonds between dead skin cells on the surface, encouraging accelerated cellular turnover. The result is a smoother texture, reduced appearance of fine lines, and a more even, radiant skin tone with consistent use.

2. Salicylic Acid (Willow Bark Extract)

As a lipid-soluble BHA, salicylic acid penetrates beyond the skin's surface and into the pore lining itself. It dissolves accumulated sebum and cellular debris — the primary causes of blackheads and breakouts. Its natural anti-inflammatory properties also help calm active blemishes and reduce post-breakout redness.

3. Kojic Acid

Kojic acid is a [clinically recognized](https://www.clinicalguidehub.online/is-clinical-cleansing-complex/) brightening agent derived from fungal fermentation. It works by inhibiting the enzyme tyrosinase, which is responsible for the production of melanin. By disrupting this process, kojic acid gradually fades existing hyperpigmentation and helps prevent new dark spots from forming.

4. Centella Asiatica

Centella Asiatica — also known as Cica or Gotu Kola — provides the critical balancing element in this formula. While the AHAs and BHAs accelerate exfoliation, Centella actively calms the skin, reduces redness, and supports the integrity of the skin's natural barrier. This prevents the irritation that can occur with acid-based serums.

5. Arbutin

Arbutin is a naturally derived brightening compound that works in synergy with kojic acid to inhibit melanin production. It is particularly effective for addressing sun-induced pigmentation and creating a more uniform skin tone over time.

How to Use iS Clinical Active Serum

For optimal results, integrate Active Serum into both your morning and evening routines:
Cleanse: Begin with a clean, dry face. Apply your cleanser, rinse, and pat dry thoroughly before applying the serum.
Apply: Dispense 2–3 drops of Active Serum into your palm or directly onto fingertips.
Press and Spread: Gently press the serum into the skin across the face and neck using upward motions. Do not rub aggressively.
Layer: Allow the serum to absorb for 60–90 seconds before applying your moisturizer or eye cream.
Sun Protection (AM): Always follow with a broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher in the morning, as exfoliating acids can increase photosensitivity.
Clinical Tip: New users should begin with once-daily evening application to allow the skin to acclimate to the acid blend. Twice-daily use can be introduced gradually over 2–3 weeks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can iS Clinical Active Serum be used on sensitive skin?
Yes, though with care. The inclusion of Centella Asiatica provides significant soothing support, making it more tolerable than many acid serums. However, sensitive skin types should begin with once-daily evening use and monitor their skin's response over the first two weeks before increasing frequency.
How long does it take to see results?
Most users begin to notice improved skin texture and a more even tone within 4–6 weeks of consistent use. Significant improvements in hyperpigmentation and fine lines typically become more visible after 8–12 weeks of twice-daily application.
Can Active Serum be used with retinol?
Using both actives simultaneously requires caution. It is generally recommended to alternate — Active Serum in the morning and retinol in the evening — or consult your dermatologist or esthetician for a personalized protocol.
Is this serum suitable for use after a chemical peel or laser treatment?
Not immediately post-procedure. iS Clinical Active Serum should only be reintroduced into the routine once the skin has fully healed and the treating physician or esthetican has cleared you to resume active ingredients — typically 7–14 days post-treatment.
